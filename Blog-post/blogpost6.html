From Floor to Ceiling: Safety-First Furniture Ideas for Renovating Every Room

Furniture Decor
18 July 2023. 6 min read

Renovating a home is an exciting yet daunting task. It's a chance to breathe new life into your living space, but it also comes with the responsibility of ensuring safety, especially if you have children or elderly family members. One often overlooked aspect of home renovation is the choice of furniture. From floor to ceiling, every piece of furniture can contribute to the safety and well-being of your household. In this blog, we'll explore some safety-focused furniture options for renovating every room in your home.

Living Room:

1. Corner Guards: Protect sharp corners of coffee tables and entertainment units with soft corner guards to prevent injuries from accidental bumps and falls.

2. Anti-Tip Furniture Straps: Secure heavy furniture like bookshelves and cabinets to the wall to prevent them from tipping over, especially in homes with young children.

3. Slip-Resistant Rugs: Use rugs with non-slip backing or rug grippers to prevent slips and falls, ensuring a safer environment, especially on hardwood or tile floors.

Kitchen:

1. Childproof Cabinet Locks: Install childproof locks on cabinets and drawers to keep hazardous items like cleaning supplies and sharp utensils out of reach of children.

2. Stove Knob Covers: Prevent accidental stove activation by using stove knob covers to safeguard against burns and potential fire hazards.

3. Adjustable Height Countertops: Consider installing countertops with adjustable heights to accommodate individuals with mobility issues, making it easier for them to work in the kitchen.

Bedroom:

1. Bed Rails: For children transitioning from a crib to a bed, bed rails provide added safety by preventing falls during sleep.

2. Night Lights: Install motion-activated night lights to illuminate pathways and reduce the risk of tripping or falling when navigating the bedroom at night.

3. Fire Escape Ladders: Keep a fire escape ladder stored under the bed or in a closet for emergency evacuation in case of a fire, especially in multi-story homes.

Bathroom:

1. Grab Bars: Install grab bars near the toilet and bathtub/shower area to assist individuals with balance and stability, reducing the risk of slips and falls.

2. Non-Slip Bath Mats: Place non-slip bath mats inside the bathtub or shower and on the floor outside to provide traction and prevent falls on wet surfaces.

3. Temperature Control Devices: Install anti-scald devices on faucets to regulate water temperature and prevent burns, particularly for households with young children or elderly individuals.

Home Office/Study:

1. Cable Management Solutions: Keep cords and cables organized and out of the way to prevent tripping hazards and potential electrical accidents.

2. Ergonomic Furniture: Invest in ergonomic chairs and desks to promote proper posture and reduce the risk of musculoskeletal strain and injury during long periods of work or study.

3. Fireproof Safe: Store important documents and valuables in a fireproof safe to protect them in the event of a fire or other emergencies.

Conclusion

When renovating your home, prioritizing safety should be at the top of your list. By carefully selecting safety-focused furniture and accessories for every room, you can create a secure and comfortable environment for your family and guests. From floor to ceiling, each piece of furniture plays a crucial role in ensuring the well-being of everyone in your household. So, as you embark on your renovation journey, remember to consider safety every step of the way.
